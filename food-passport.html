JPN
2026
Osaka, Japan — March 2026

Tonkotsu ramen at a 12-seat counter

Dotonbori backstreet

Broth simmered for two days, noodles ordered "kata" (firm). Ate it standing at a counter that only sat twelve people, queue out the door by 7pm.

THA
2025
Chiang Mai, Thailand — November 2025

Khao soi from a family stall

Wararot Market

Crispy noodles on top, soft ones underneath, a curry broth that took three days to build heat tolerance for. Worth every bit of sweat.

ITA
2025
Bologna, Italy — September 2025

Tagliatelle al ragù, the real one

A trattoria with no sign out front

No cream, no garlic, just meat, wine, and time. Finally understood why Bolognese abroad rarely tastes like this.

MEX
2025
Mexico City — July 2025

Tacos al pastor off a spinning trompo

La Condesa, after midnight

Pineapple shaved straight onto the meat as it came off the spit. Ate five and regretted none of them.

VNM
2024
Hoi An, Vietnam — December 2024

Bánh mì from the same cart since 1990

Old Town corner stall

Pâté, pickled daikon, coriander, a chilli that snuck up two bites in. The baguette shattered exactly the way it should.

GRC
2024
Naxos, Greece — August 2024

Grilled octopus, straight off the line

A taverna on the harbour

Watched it get pulled from a drying line above the door before it hit the grill. Char, olive oil, lemon, nothing else needed.

The idea behind this passport

This is a running log of meals worth remembering — not restaurant reviews, just honest notes on what I ate, where, and why it stuck with me. Started as a way to keep track of dishes I wanted to recreate at home; turned into something worth sharing.

New entries get added as I travel. Everything here is written after the fact, from memory and a phone full of photos — so expect a bit of bias toward the meals that were actually good.
